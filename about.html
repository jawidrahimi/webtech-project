What OLAC Does

The Open Language Archives Community (OLAC) is an international partnership of institutions and individuals who are creating a worldwide virtual library of language resources. OLAC provides infrastructure, standards, and tools to support the digital archiving of language data.

  • Establishes metadata standards for language resource description.
  • Supports interoperability through the OAI Protocol for Metadata Harvesting.
  • Promotes access to endangered language materials and promotes long-term preservation.
  • Offers searchable services through the OLAC Search Portal.
  • Encourages contributions from archives worldwide via its Tools and registration process.

Learn more about OLAC's approach and technologies in the full Overview Document.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is OLAC?

OLAC is a network of language archives and services built around shared metadata standards and protocols.

Is OLAC a data archive?

No. OLAC is not an archive itself, but a registry and aggregator of metadata from other archives.

How can I register my archive?

You can register by following the guidelines in the Tools section, and submitting metadata in OLAC format.

Is there a search interface?

Yes. OLAC provides a faceted search tool accessible from here.

Who maintains OLAC?

OLAC is maintained by a community of volunteer contributors, led by linguists and technologists worldwide.

What kind of resources are included?

Resources include grammars, dictionaries, audio recordings, video documentation, corpora, and more.
